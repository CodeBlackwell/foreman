# Scope

This specification defines the dimensional tolerances, inspection criteria, and post-repair verification requirements for the {{Machine:X200 Press}}. All inspections must be performed by a qualified quality control technician using calibrated measurement instruments.

Instruments must be calibrated within 12 months of use. Out-of-calibration instruments invalidate inspection results.

# Dimensional Tolerances

## Drive Shaft Runout

Maximum allowable {{Term:Runout}} for the X200 drive shaft is 0.025 mm TIR (Total Indicator Reading), measured at the drive-end bearing seat. Measurements exceeding this value require the shaft to be straightened or replaced before the press returns to service.

Measure runout with a dial indicator mounted on a magnetic base, rotating the shaft by hand through a minimum of two full revolutions.

## Bearing Housing Tolerance

The {{Part:Bearing Housing}} bore must conform to the following tolerances:

Bore diameter: 75.000 mm +0.025 / -0.000 mm (H7 fit)
Roundness: maximum 0.010 mm
Cylindricity: maximum 0.015 mm over the full bearing seat length

Measurements outside these tolerances indicate housing wear or damage. The housing must be replaced or reconditioned before a new bearing is installed. Installing a bearing in an out-of-tolerance housing will reduce bearing life significantly and may cause premature failure.

# Post-Repair Inspection

Perform the following checks after every bearing replacement or drive assembly repair on the {{Machine:X200 Press}} before the equipment is returned to production.

1. Confirm that lockout/tagout has been fully removed and the equipment has been restored per the safety procedure before connecting power. Do not operate the press with any LOTO devices in place.

2. Run the press at no-load for 10 minutes. Record bearing temperature at the start and end of the run using a calibrated contact thermometer. Temperature rise must not exceed 15 degrees C above ambient within the first 10 minutes.

3. Measure drive shaft {{Term:Runout}} with the press at operating temperature. Must be within specification.

4. Verify all fastener torque values against the maintenance manual specification.
[REFERENCES: DOC-002 §Torque Specifications]

5. Record all measurements in the equipment inspection log. Include instrument serial numbers, calibration dates, technician name, and date of inspection. The inspection log entry is the release authorization for the equipment to return to production.

{{Part:Bearing Housing}} dimensional check must be repeated if the press is involved in any overload event exceeding 120% of rated press force.
