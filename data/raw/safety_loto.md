# Scope

This procedure establishes the minimum requirements for the control of hazardous energy during servicing and maintenance of machinery and equipment. It applies to all personnel performing maintenance, repair, or adjustment on equipment where unexpected energization or startup could cause injury.

All employees working on or near {{Machine:X200 Press}} and similar machinery must follow this procedure without exception. Failure to comply is a serious safety violation.

# Lockout Procedure

The following steps must be performed in sequence before any maintenance work begins on equipment with {{Hazard:Stored Energy}}.

1. Notify all affected employees that servicing or maintenance is required and that equipment must be shut down and locked out.

2. Identify all energy sources for the {{Machine:X200 Press}}, including electrical, pneumatic, hydraulic, mechanical, gravitational, and thermal sources.

3. Shut down the equipment using the normal stopping procedure (e.g., power off switch or stop button).

4. Isolate the equipment from all energy sources using the appropriate isolation devices (main disconnect switch, valve, block, etc.).

5. Apply an individual {{Part:Lockout Device}} to each energy isolating device. Each authorized employee must apply their own lock. {{Term:LOTO}} compliance requires one lock per person.

6. Release, restrain, or otherwise render safe all potential residual or stored energy. Bleed down pneumatic lines, discharge capacitors, block elevated machine members.

7. Verify de-energization by attempting to start the equipment using the normal operating controls. If the equipment does not start, de-energization is confirmed.

[REFERENCES: DOC-003 §Post-Repair Inspection]

# Tagout Procedure

Where lockout is not feasible due to equipment design, tagout may be used with additional safeguards. Tags must be securely attached to each energy isolating device. Tags must include the employee name, department, and date applied.

Tags are warning devices only and do not provide the physical restraint of a lock. Additional means of ensuring de-energization must be implemented when using tagout.

# Restoration Procedure

Before restoring equipment to service after maintenance is complete, all authorized employees must remove their own locks and tags in reverse order. Verify that all tools, materials, and personnel are clear of the equipment before energizing.

Conduct a post-restoration check per {{Term:LOTO}} reinstatement requirements. See quality control post-repair inspection requirements.
