# General Maintenance Schedule

The {{Machine:X200 Press}} requires scheduled preventive maintenance to ensure safe and reliable operation. All maintenance personnel must be trained and authorized before performing any service on this equipment.

Maintenance intervals: daily visual inspection, weekly lubrication check, monthly bearing inspection, annual overhaul. All maintenance activities must be logged in the equipment service record.

# Bearing Replacement Procedure

Replace the {{Part:Drive Bearing}} when vibration exceeds 5 mm/s RMS, when bearing temperature exceeds 80 degrees C under normal load, or when inspection reveals pitting, spalling, or unusual wear. Estimated time: 4 hours.

1. Review the complete procedure before beginning work. Gather all required tools: bearing puller, press tool, torque wrench, dial indicator, cleaning solvent, and new {{Part:Drive Bearing}} matching OEM specification.

2. De-energize the {{Machine:X200 Press}} and apply lockout/tagout before removing any guards or accessing the drive assembly.
[REQUIRES_SAFETY: DOC-001 §Lockout Procedure]

3. Remove the drive-end guard by loosening the six M8 bolts in a cross pattern. Set the guard aside on a clean surface.

4. Use the bearing puller to extract the worn {{Part:Drive Bearing}} from the {{Part:Bearing Housing}}. Apply steady, even force. Do not use heat on the housing. Record the removal torque if unusual resistance is observed.

5. Clean the {{Part:Bearing Housing}} bore with solvent. Inspect for scoring, corrosion, or out-of-round condition. Measure the bore diameter with an internal micrometer and compare to the tolerance specification.

6. Press the new {{Part:Drive Bearing}} into the {{Part:Bearing Housing}} using the press tool, applying force only to the outer race. Ensure the bearing seats fully and squarely. Do not strike the bearing directly with a hammer.

7. Reinstall the drive-end guard. Torque the M8 bolts to specification. See torque table below.
[VALIDATES_WITH: DOC-003 §Bearing Housing Tolerance]

8. Remove lockout/tagout devices per the restoration procedure. Restore energy and run the press through one complete cycle at reduced load. Monitor bearing temperature and vibration for the first 30 minutes of operation.

# Lubrication Points

The {{Machine:X200 Press}} has four grease fittings that require lubrication every 250 operating hours. Use NLGI Grade 2 grease only. Over-lubrication can cause seal failure.

Fitting locations: drive-end bearing (1), idler-end bearing (1), crankshaft journal (2). Apply grease until fresh grease appears at the seal lip, then wipe away excess.

# Torque Specifications

{{Term:Torque}} values below are for dry threads unless noted. Always use a calibrated torque wrench.

| Fastener | Torque (Nm) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Drive-end guard M8 bolts | 25 | Dry, cross pattern |
| Bearing housing M12 bolts | 65 | Loctite 243 required |
| Crankshaft retaining nut M24 | 180 | Wet with oil |
